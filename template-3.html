Quick answers,
fewer phone calls.

The questions we hear most often — answered upfront so you can plan your visit with confidence.

Do I need a referral to see a nephrologist? +
Most insurance plans require a referral from your primary care physician. We'll help coordinate this — just give us a call and we'll walk you through it.
What should I bring to my first appointment? +
A current medication list (including over-the-counter and supplements), recent lab work if available, your insurance card, and a photo ID. We'll handle the rest.
How early should my labs be done? +
Ideally within 1–2 weeks before your appointment. Kidney function can change, so recent labs give us the most accurate picture of your health.
Which insurance plans do you accept? +
We accept most major insurance carriers including BlueCross BlueShield, Aetna, Cigna, UnitedHealthcare, Medicare, Medicaid, Humana, and Tricare. Call us if you don't see yours listed.
Can I do dialysis at home? +
Yes — we offer comprehensive home dialysis training and ongoing support. Many of our patients successfully manage their treatment from the comfort of their own homes.
